# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Product engineers and design-minded development teams evaluating or integrating a dependable voice layer into a web product. They need to understand the model quickly, see it work in a realistic product, and trust that actions stay within developer-defined routes and workflows.

## Product Purpose

Lexicon turns a product's explicit structure into a reliable voice interface. The landing experience should make deterministic mapping feel tangible, lead visitors into the working Rillwork demo, and distinguish Lexicon from browser agents that rediscover or visually scrape an interface on every run.

## Brand Personality

Precise, assured, and quietly kinetic. Technical detail should feel legible and intentional, while the visual system stays distinctive through graphite surfaces, acid-lime signal color, and controlled motion.

## Anti-references

Generic magical-assistant imagery, undirected neon spectacle, oversized effects that crowd the message, simulated automation, and familiar SaaS card-template layouts. Motion should never feel trapped in its frame or more important than the product claim.

## Design Principles

- Make the product model visible instead of asking visitors to trust a claim.
- Let one clear signal color communicate activity, authority, and state.
- Keep visual effects spacious and self-contained so they support the reading flow.
- Demonstrate deterministic execution through real mapped interactions.
- Prefer technical clarity and restraint over decorative complexity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast and keyboard access. Preserve visible focus states, semantic controls, responsive layouts, and a reduced-motion experience that remains visually complete.
