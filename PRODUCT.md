# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Lexicon is for software teams embedding dependable voice interaction into existing products. Developers use the SDK to expose typed capabilities, while product teams and evaluators use the Rillwork demo and console to understand, test, and operate those capabilities through familiar application workflows.

## Product Purpose

Lexicon turns product-defined actions, schemas, reads, and confirmation policies into a reusable voice-agent SDK. Success means voice and manual interfaces can invoke the same guarded product capabilities without brittle browser automation, improvised clicks, or duplicated business logic.

## Brand Personality

Technical, precise, and confident. The product should feel engineered rather than theatrical: direct language, clear hierarchy, restrained motion, and an unmistakable acid-green signal used only where action or state deserves attention.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS card grids, excessive rounding, decorative borders, filler copy, cramped layouts, glass effects, disconnected demo logic, and chat interfaces appearing where normal forms or standard product controls are expected.

## Design Principles

1. Show the capability model through working product behavior.
2. Keep voice interaction distinct while sharing the same SDK execution path.
3. Prefer familiar application controls for manual work.
4. Use spacing and hierarchy before adding visual chrome.
5. Make safety and confirmation explicit without slowing down routine actions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Maintain readable contrast, keyboard-operable controls, clear focus states, semantic dialogs and forms, responsive layouts, and reduced-motion alternatives. Do not communicate status through color alone.
